STEM Classification Fields

2 variables

stem_occupation - STEM Occupation Category

High-level classification of whether a position is categorized as STEM, Health, or other occupation types. Useful for analyzing the federal government's science and technology workforce.

All Values

ALL OTHER OCCUPATIONS (72.8%) STEM OCCUPATIONS (18.4%) HEALTH OCCUPATIONS (8.7%) UNSPECIFIED

Potential Analysis Questions

  • Which agencies have the highest concentration of STEM workers?
  • How has STEM employment changed over time across the federal government?
  • What is the separation rate for STEM vs non-STEM positions?
  • Are STEM positions more likely to be filled by new hires or transfers?

stem_occupation_type - Detailed STEM Category

More granular breakdown of STEM occupations into specific disciplines. Enables analysis of engineering, technology, science, and math workforce segments.

All Values

ALL OTHER OCCUPATIONS ENGINEERING OCCUPATIONS TECHNOLOGY OCCUPATIONS SCIENCE OCCUPATIONS MATHEMATICS OCCUPATIONS HEALTH OCCUPATIONS UNSPECIFIED

Potential Analysis Questions

  • Which STEM discipline has the highest representation in federal employment?
  • How do salaries compare across STEM disciplines?
  • What is the age distribution within each STEM category?
  • Which agencies are actively recruiting for specific STEM disciplines?

Supervisory Status Fields

2 variables

supervisory_status - Supervisory Classification

Indicates whether an employee holds a supervisory, managerial, team leader, or non-supervisory position. Critical for understanding organizational structure and leadership distribution.

All Values

ALL OTHER POSITIONS (84%) SUPERVISOR OR MANAGER (11.7%) TEAM LEADER LEADER SUPERVISOR (CSRA) MANAGEMENT OFFICIAL (CSRA)

Potential Analysis Questions

  • What is the supervisor-to-employee ratio across agencies?
  • How does supervisory status correlate with salary and grade?
  • Are supervisors more or less likely to separate than non-supervisors?
  • How has the management layer changed over time?

supervisory_status_code - Supervisory Code

Numeric code representation of supervisory status. Values range from 2-8, with mean of 7.21 indicating most employees are non-supervisory.

Values

2 (Supervisor/Mgr) 4 5 6 7 8 (Non-supervisory)

Tenure Fields

1 variable

length_of_service_years - Federal Service Tenure

Continuous numeric field representing years of federal service. Ranges from 0 to 72.5 years with mean of 11.94 years for current employees. Separations show higher mean (18.54 years) due to retirements.

Statistics

Min: 0.0 years Max: 72.5 years Mean (Employment): 11.94 Mean (Accessions): 7.22 Mean (Separations): 18.54

Potential Analysis Questions

  • What is the average tenure by agency or occupational series?
  • How does tenure correlate with grade level and salary?
  • At what tenure length do employees most commonly separate?
  • Which agencies have the most experienced workforce?
  • How does new hire tenure (prior federal service) vary by accession type?

Dynamics-Specific Fields

4 variables

accession_category / accession_category_code

Categorizes the type of entry into federal employment. Distinguishes between competitive vs excepted service appointments, SES appointments, and transfers.

Categories

AA - NEW HIRE - COMPETITIVE SERVICE AC - NEW HIRE - EXCEPTED SERVICE AD - NEW HIRE - SES AE - TRANSFER IN

Potential Analysis Questions

  • What proportion of new hires come through competitive vs excepted service?
  • How has the balance of hiring paths changed over time?
  • Which agencies rely most on transfers vs external hiring?

separation_category / separation_category_code

Categorizes the type of departure from federal employment. Critical for understanding workforce attrition patterns.

Categories

SB - QUIT (Resignation) SG - RETIREMENT - VOLUNTARY SJ - TRANSFER OUT SH - TERMINATION SA - OTHER SEPARATION SE - RETIREMENT - OTHER SD - RETIREMENT - EARLY OUT SC - REDUCTION IN FORCE (RIF) SL - MASS TRANSFER

Potential Analysis Questions

  • What is the breakdown of voluntary vs involuntary separations?
  • How do separation reasons vary by age group?
  • Which agencies have the highest resignation rates?
  • How have RIF numbers changed during workforce reduction periods?
